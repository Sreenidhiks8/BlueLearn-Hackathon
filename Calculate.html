1. CRYPTOCURRENCY

What is cryptocurrency? It is a virtual (or) a digital currency and can also be comprehended as an ASSET. Since it is a digital currency it can be used for transaction while buying a good, for example. Cryptocurrency is a digital payment system that doesn't rely on banks to verify transactions. Cryptocurrency is stored in digital wallets. When you transfer cryptocurrency funds, the transactions are recorded in a public ledger.

 PROS:-

1) No fraud and scam - no manipulation within cryptocurrency although being aware of scammers depends on a person.

2) Ownership is extremely fast and immediate - no paper works during transaction (or) transferring of ownership

3) Secure Ownership - transferring of ownership to another person needs a PRIVATE KEY.

4) No personal information leakage - we provide certain information to a merchant while buying online goods but it's not the same case with cryptocurrency transaction.

 CONS:-

1) Loss risk is high - crypto market is decentralized and no authority owns it. So if there is any problem, for example, in transaction, you can't claim to anyone.

2) Market is volatile - price value of a crypto varies heavily with time and needs good knowledge and research to back your investments

2. STOCK MARKET

Feel like heard it somewhere before? Yes the popular stock market. You have seen in the news, heard from your neighbours. Oh don't know what stock market is? Well stock market is a place where a person can buy and sell STOCKS of public listed companies are traded  

What is a stock? 

A stock is a financial instrument that represents ownership in a company or corporation and represents a proportionate claim on its assets (what it owns) and earnings (what it generates in profits). Stocks are also called shares or a company's equity.

 

PROS:-

1) Capital Gains - if invested wisely, a single stock can change your lifestyle.

2) Choice of investing in various stocks - can choose to invest in multinational companies or small scale buisnesses depending on your choice.

3) Ease of transaction - buying a stock and selling it is safer than buying, for example, real estate.

CONS:-

1) Capital loss - High risk high reward.

2) Transaction is costly - need to pay a fee for every transaction.

 3. SAVINGS ACCOUNT and/or FIXED DEPOSIT

Savings account:-

Well you might probably have a savings account already. But if you don't then making one in a bank of your choice would be a good idea. The basic idea behind a savings account is the bank, whichever you choose, has an interest rate that it sums up to the amount you have deposited.

Fixed deposits:-

Fixed deposits, as the name suggest, is a type of investment in which you give a certain amount of money to the bank for a certain period of time. The period varies from a minimum - days count, to years. The higher the tenure, the higher the interest you will get. In this way you get a certain interest rate higher than that of a savings account and the rate depends on the bank you choose. But the drawback is if you withdraw before the maturity of the funds i.e the full extent of the time period, you would have to pay a penalty fee. So it is advisable to stay patient and reep the rewards wisely.
